OKRs for Product Teams: Beyond the Buzzword

By Brett Flügel June 28, 2024

Objectives and Key Results (OKRs) have become the go-to goal-setting framework for product teams everywhere. Yet for every success story, there are countless teams struggling with OKRs that feel disconnected from reality, impossible to measure, or completely ignored by the end of the quarter.

The problem isn't with OKRs themselves—it's with how most teams implement them. After working with dozens of product teams on their OKR practices, I've identified the patterns that separate successful OKR implementations from the failures.

The Common OKR Mistakes

Before diving into solutions, let's address the most common mistakes I see:

1. Treating OKRs as Project Plans

Many teams confuse OKRs with project plans, creating key results like "Launch feature X" or "Complete user research study." These aren't outcomes—they're outputs. True key results measure the impact you expect to achieve.

2. Setting Too Many OKRs

Focus is the enemy of too many priorities. Teams that set 5+ objectives per quarter often achieve none of them well. Limit yourself to 3-5 key results per objective, and 2-3 objectives maximum per quarter.

3. Making Key Results Binary

Key results should be measurable and have a clear scale of success. "Improve user satisfaction" is too vague. "Increase NPS from 32 to 45" gives you something concrete to work toward and measure progress against.

The Anatomy of Great Product OKRs

Effective product OKRs follow a specific structure:

Objectives Should Be:

  • Inspirational: Something that motivates the team
  • Qualitative: Describes the desired outcome, not how to get there
  • Time-bound: Achievable within the quarter
  • Actionable: Something the team can directly influence

Key Results Should Be:

  • Specific and measurable: Clear metrics with target values
  • Outcome-focused: Measure impact, not activity
  • Achievable but aggressive: 70% confidence level is ideal
  • Limited in number: 3-5 per objective maximum

Example: Well-Crafted Product OKRs

Objective: Dramatically improve new user activation

Key Results:

  • Increase day-7 retention rate from 25% to 40%
  • Reduce time-to-first-value from 3 days to 24 hours
  • Achieve 8+ NPS score from users in their first week

The OKR Process That Actually Works

Setting OKRs is just the beginning. Here's the process that ensures they drive results:

1. Weekly Check-ins

Don't wait until the end of the quarter to see if you're on track. Weekly 15-minute check-ins keep OKRs top of mind and allow for course corrections.

2. Confidence Tracking

Track your confidence level (1-10) for achieving each key result. If confidence drops below 5, it's time to adjust tactics or reset expectations.

3. Learning Documentation

Whether you hit 100% or 30% of your targets, document what you learned. This creates institutional knowledge and improves future OKR setting.

Making OKRs Stick

The most common reason OKRs fail is lack of adoption. Here are tactics to make them stick:

  • Connect daily work to OKRs: Every major project should clearly connect to a key result
  • Celebrate progress: Acknowledge wins along the way, not just final results
  • Use them in decision-making: When prioritizing features, always ask "which option best serves our OKRs?"
  • Make them visible: Display current OKRs and progress in team spaces and dashboards

Remember: OKRs are a tool, not a destination. They work best when they help teams focus on what matters most and create alignment around measurable outcomes. When implemented thoughtfully, they transform how product teams operate and dramatically improve their results.
